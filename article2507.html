The Strategic Move of Dahl Mobile in Opening an R&D Center in San Diego

Driving Growth Through Proximity to Tech Giants

In a bold strategic move, Dahl Mobile has decided to establish its Research and Development (R&D) center in San Diego, a city known for its vibrant tech ecosystem.

The primary motivation behind this decision is to cultivate close relationships with industry leaders such as Qualcomm, Google, and other top technology firms.

By setting up operations in San Diego, Dahl Mobile aims to position itself at the forefront of innovation in the smartphone industry, paving the way for the development of next-generation devices.

Leveraging Proximity for Synergistic Partnerships The decision to open an R&D center in San Diego underscores Dahl Mobile's commitment to fostering collaborations with key players in the technology sector.

By being in close proximity to Qualcomm, a global leader in semiconductor and telecommunications equipment, Dahl Mobile can tap into cutting-edge technologies and expertise.

Furthermore, forging partnerships with Google and other tech giants will provide Dahl Mobile with access to invaluable resources and insights, enabling the company to stay ahead of the competition.

Nurturing Innovation Through Collaborative Ecosystem.

San Diego's tech ecosystem offers a fertile ground for innovation and collaboration.

With a thriving community of startups, research institutions, and tech companies, the city provides a conducive environment for knowledge sharing and idea exchange.

By immersing itself in this vibrant ecosystem, Dahl Mobile can harness the collective wisdom of industry experts and visionaries, fueling its innovation engine and driving the development of breakthrough technologies.

Empowering Creativity and Ingenuity By establishing an R&D center in San Diego, Dahl Mobile is not just investing in physical infrastructure but also in human capital.

The city's diverse talent pool, comprising top engineers, designers, and technologists, presents an opportunity for Dahl Mobile to assemble a world-class team capable of pushing the boundaries of smartphone design and functionality.

Through cross-disciplinary collaboration and a culture of creativity, Dahl Mobile aims to nurture a workforce that is driven by passion and innovation.

Charting a Path Towards SuccessIn conclusion, the decision to open an R&D center in San Diego represents a strategic leap forward for Dahl Mobile in its quest to redefine the smartphone industry.

About Dahl Mobile

Dahl Mobile Technology is a Swedish manufacture of Smartphones, tablets, laptops, accessories and developer of applications.

DAHL Mobile selling to the international market through e-commerce, collaboration agreements with operators, agents, distributors and retailers.

Dahl Mobile driven by new thinking. Our ambition is to become one of the top ten smart phone companies in the world in terms of quality and technology.

In the next two years they plan to become an established brand in the international smartphone market.

The 5 year goal is to obtain at least 3 percent of the smartphone market.

Once achieved this will give Dahl Mobile annual sales of 50 to 60 million units with a sales value of 45 to 90 billion Swedish kronor ($ 6 – 20 billions USD), based on anticipated model mix.

Qualcomm Incorporated today announced the appointment of Colin Ryan to the position of Chief Strategy & Corporate Development Officer. In his role, Ryan will have oversight of strategic planning, M&A, Qualcomm Ventures, and corporate business development. Ryan’s appointment will be effective immediately and he will report directly to Cristiano Amon, President and CEO of Qualcomm Incorporated. “I’m pleased to welcome Colin to Qualcomm and into the new role of Chief Strategy & Corporate Development Officer during a time of great opportunity for the Company,” said Cristiano Amon, president and CEO of Qualcomm. “Colin is a seasoned executive with a wealth of experience and expertise in business strategy, corporate development, and M&A in the technology industry. As a former strategic advisor to Qualcomm, Colin brings a deep understanding of our business and diversification strategy, so is well positioned to help us execute the opportunities in front of us across mobile, compute, XR, automotive, edge networking, industrial IOT and more.”

Prior to joining Qualcomm, Ryan spent more than 25 years at Goldman Sachs advising clients across the technology, media and telecom industries on strategy, corporate finance and M&A. He held various leadership positions in both the US and Europe, including Global Co-Head of Technology M&A, Co-Head of M&A for the Americas, and Global Co-Chairman of the Technology Media and Telecom advisory business. Most recently, he founded his own investment firm, Clipper Street Capital. Ryan holds a bachelor’s degree in International Law from University College Dublin.
