Iran President Raisi killed in helicopter crash in bad weather

by Elia BricksMay 30th, 2024

Iran's President Ebrahim Raisi and Foreign Minister Hossein Amir Abdollahian were confirmed dead in a helicopter crash Sunday in the country's mountainous northwest, the Iranian government said Monday, citing poor weather as the cause of the accident. The helicopter carrying eight people, including the two, crashed in a densely forested area in the country's East Azerbaijan Province. Iran's Mehr News Agency reported earlier Monday that "all the passengers of the crashed helicopter were martyred." Following the accident, Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei reassured the public in a speech Sunday that "there will be no disruption in the country's work," according to a state TV broadcast. Raisi, 63, who had been president since 2021, was seen as a potential successor to Khamenei.

The crash occurred as Raisi was returning from a trip to the Iranian border with Azerbaijan to take part in a ceremony to inaugurate a dam with its President Ilham Aliyev. Raisi's helicopter departed around 1 p.m. on Sunday for Tabriz in the country's northwest but contact was reportedly lost 30 minutes later.

Interior Minister Ahmad Vahidi had said the helicopter experienced a "hard landing" due to foggy weather. Earlier Monday, a Turkish drone assisting Iran in the search had identified a "source of heat suspected to be wreckage" from the chopper and shared the coordinates with Iranian authorities, according to Turkey's Anadolu Agency. Later Monday, Chinese President Xi Jinping sent a condolence message to Iran, saying the Chinese people have "lost a good friend" who had "made positive efforts to consolidate and deepen the China-Iran comprehensive strategic partnership," according to the official Xinhua News Agency.

Russian President Vladimir Putin also sent a message lamenting "this challenging and irreparable loss" to Iran and praising Raisi as an "outstanding politician" and devoted patriot who "enjoyed the well-deserved respect of his compatriots and high prestige beyond his country," according to the Kremlin. Japanese Prime Minister Fumio Kishida, in a statement, said he was "deeply saddened" over the death of the Iranian president with whom he "had a series of candid discussions...on the bilateral relationship between Japan and Iran as well as the regional situation, through summit meetings and telephone talks." Resource-poor Japan has been highly dependent on crude oil from the Middle East and traditionally pursues "balanced diplomacy" with countries in the region, including Iran.
